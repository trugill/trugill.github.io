About Me

I am a dedicated researcher and developer with a passion for herpetology, ornithology, and geospatial sciences. My work focuses on spatial ecology, where I strive to make an impact on understanding and conserving wildlife through the integration of field research, data analysis, and geospatial technology. By combining ecological principles with modern analytical tools, I contribute to research that promotes better conservation methods and fosters a better understanding of the natural world.

Beyond spatial ecology, I love hands-on work with animals. During the summer and winter breaks, I work as a veterinary assistant or technician in the DFW area. During the semester, I enjoy volunteering for research programs to get my hands dirty outside of Brazos county. Additionally, I have an affinity for electrical engineering and love designing circuits, 3D printing, and coding.

Research & Lab Involvement

Research Interests

Spatial Ecology

The main focus of my research is spatial ecology. Some projects of mine include utilizing Maxent to project and approximate the potential range of invasive species. My work involves a combination of ArcGIS, ENMTools, R, and Maxent for the best estimation of habitat suitability for any given species. For the models you see from me, these maps represent the fundamental niche, not the realized niche. Other than that, I also love using GIS softwares to visualize data. You can see some of the maps I've made towards the bottom of this page!

Coding

I've been coding since I've entered high school. I've had a range of projects over the year, including creating Minesweeper and solitaire from scratch on Java, as well as writing an ImageJ script to speed up the process of analyzing frog movement distances up to six times faster than the original, hand-measuring method! As of recently, I've been working on an R program to help researchers without GIS knowledge to map theoretical niches. Let me ask you this: how many other undergraduates do you know have their own websites?

Electrical Engineering

Although I'm not an engineer, I went to an engineering high school and am passionate about circuitry. I consider myself an electronics hobbyist and now apply my skills to my research. In the past, I've developed products such as a remote-controlled hummingbird trap and a handheld GIS device. I frequently use my skills in 3D modeling/printing for producing lab equipment. I hope to be able to design research-grade tools one day.
